Modern Tales of Mountains and Rivers

At a weekend, I visited South Anhui province with my family. The majestic Mount Huang in my hometown prompted me to look up Chinese Classic of Mountains and Rivers for relative record about it. However, rather than being impressed by the little record about Mount Huang in this book, what fascinated me was its description of strange animals: for example, Qiong Qi, the evil spirit specializing in disdaining the goodness and celebrating evilness. He was known as the "cator of the virtuous souls", whose body was finally caten by "Tao Tie", another mythical ferocious animal; "Da Ni", which could rejuvenate the dead wood and sustain youth; and "Jingwei Bird" which unremittingly filled the sea with land.

It's impossible to find the strange animals and beasts in real world. Though the real "Qiong Qi" and "Tao Tie" had disappeared, it is not difficult to find their traits and qualities in the modern and contemporary society.
